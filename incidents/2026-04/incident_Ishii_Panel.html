Sugar Tax Panel — Credential Misrepresentation and Duty of Care
Sources: Berkeley Municipal Code §7.72.150; San Francisco Chronicle, April 22, 2026;
Berkeley corridor analysis; 2024 mayoral candidate materials
Adena Ishii · Mayor (formerly Council District 1)
What Happened

The Sugar-Sweetened Beverage Product Panel of Experts was established by Berkeley's soda tax ballot measure to provide independent oversight of how soda tax revenues are spent. Berkeley Municipal Code §7.72.150 specifies that members must hold professional backgrounds in nutrition science, public health research, or medical practice. Adena Ishii — whose professional background is in nonprofit consulting and community organizing, with degrees in business (UC Berkeley Haas) and law (Santa Clara University) — was appointed to the panel. None of these qualifications meet the credential requirements the ordinance specifies.

Ishii subsequently cited her appointment in her 2024 mayoral campaign materials as evidence of "ten years of local policy experience." The appointment served as a credential — signaling policy authority she had not earned through the expertise the role was designed to require.

During her tenure on the panel, Berkeley Youth Alternatives received annual soda tax grants for its Urban Agriculture and Team Nutrition Program. The panel's function implies ongoing accountability: recommending programs for funding and verifying that funded programs deliver what was promised. An April 2025 county evaluation later documented significant performance failures in BYA's work under a separate cannabis education contract, raising questions about BYA's organizational capacity that would have been relevant to any body overseeing BYA-administered programs.

Ishii left the panel upon becoming Mayor in January 2025 and bears no responsibility for the June 2025 reauthorization vote. Her accountability question is narrower: what performance verification, if any, did the panel conduct of BYA's soda tax programs during her time as a member?

Panel Credential Integrity
−5

A body called the Panel of Experts carries an implied representation that its members possess the expertise needed to evaluate program quality in the relevant domain. The municipal code makes that representation explicit with credential requirements. Ishii's appointment did not meet those requirements. Her presence on the panel did not add expert scrutiny; it added political legitimacy — which is a different and lesser thing.

The additional weight in this score — beyond what an unqualified appointment would warrant on its own — reflects the active use Ishii made of the appointment. She did not passively accept a role she was unfit for. She cited the appointment in her campaign for higher office as evidence of the policy expertise the credential requirements were designed to signal. That is an instrumentalization of a governance mechanism, not merely an oversight in her résumé.

Duty of Care: Ongoing Performance Verification
−6

A grant oversight panel that recommends programs and does not subsequently verify their performance is not providing accountability — it is providing a single gate with nothing behind it. BYA was receiving soda tax funds during Ishii's panel tenure. Whether the panel reviewed those programs' outcomes in any meaningful way is a factual question that panel meeting records would answer.

The accountability failure here is not that Ishii should have known about the 2025 cannabis contract evaluation, which was completed after she left. It is that the panel she served on appears to have operated without the ongoing verification function that is its only legitimate reason to exist. A member who could not have been providing expert oversight — because she did not hold the expertise the role required — also could not have been conducting meaningful program review. The credential failure and the oversight failure are the same failure, expressed twice.

Pending verification: Panel meeting records from Ishii's tenure would either confirm or substantially revise this score. If records show the panel conducted substantive BYA performance reviews during her service, the Duty of Care score will be revised upward. If records show the panel did not meet, met without reviewing BYA, or delegated review to staff without panel oversight, the score stands. Dates of Ishii's panel tenure also require confirmation.
