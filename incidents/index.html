The incident record captures specific accountability events that cannot be measured by transcript keyword analysis alone — conflicts of interest, credential misrepresentation, contractor oversight failures, and responses to documented wrongdoing. Each incident is scored on named dimensions with full reasoning. Scores are subject to revision as pending verifications are resolved.

Incident scores feed into the Character & Conduct pillar and the separate Incident Record pillar on individual scorecards. Positive incidents — members who proactively called for accountability — are recorded here as well.

Bartlett — Household Entanglement with City Subcontractor
Documented April 22, 2026 · Source: SF Chronicle · Ben Bartlett, District 3
Bartlett's wife co-incorporated Upline Solutions, which received city money as a subcontractor on a $607K cannabis education contract that failed to deliver its core obligations. No public disclosure of the household connection was identified. No recusal from related votes was identified. When asked about accountability, Bartlett proposed no review and suggested contractors might yet fulfill obligations from a closed grant period.

Ishii — Sugar Tax Panel Credential Misrepresentation and Duty of Care
Period of concern: Ishii's panel tenure (dates TBC) · Adena Ishii, Mayor (formerly D1)
Ishii was appointed to the Sugar-Sweetened Beverage Product Panel of Experts despite holding no credential in nutrition science, public health research, or medical practice — the backgrounds required by BMC §7.72.150. She subsequently cited the appointment in her 2024 mayoral campaign materials as evidence of policy expertise. During her tenure, BYA received panel-recommended soda tax grants; whether the panel conducted substantive performance review is unverified.
