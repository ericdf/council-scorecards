Berkeley City Council — Incident Record
All documented accountability incidents · Scores decay 20% annually in the absence of similar incidents

What is an incident? An incident is a documented accountability event that cannot be captured by automated transcript or voting analysis alone. Incidents can arise from any verifiable source — meeting transcripts, constituent communications, news coverage, court filings, public records requests, or other public documents. Each incident is scored on named dimensions; each dimension carries a pillar tag indicating which aspect of a council member's performance it implicates (Character & Conduct or Fiscal Stewardship). Scores are editorial assessments grounded in documented facts. Reasonable people may weigh the same facts differently; the reasoning behind each score is shown in full on the incident page. Incidents can be positive as well as negative — a member who proactively exposes a problem or demands accountability earns points here.
