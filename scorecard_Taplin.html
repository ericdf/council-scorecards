Agenda Behavior — Consent & Action CalendarsOfficial record
Berkeley’s council agenda is divided into two calendars. The consent calendar is a list of items voted on together as a single block, with no individual debate — a routine mechanism for non-controversial matters. The action calendar contains items debated and voted on individually. Any councilmember can request that a consent item be moved to the action calendar, forcing a real discussion before a vote. This score tracks whether members use that power — and whether they sponsor items on the consent calendar that deserve more scrutiny than a block vote allows.
